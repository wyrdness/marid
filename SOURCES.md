## Note on Sources

This documentation draws from:
- **Islamic religious texts** — Primary theological sources
- **Academic scholarship** — Peer-reviewed research on Islamic theology and Arabian folklore
- **Historical literature** — Arabian Nights and related texts
- **Anthropological studies** — Field research on contemporary djinn beliefs
- **Popular culture** — Western adaptations and interpretations

When studying Marid and djinn, it is important to distinguish between:
1. Islamic theological perspectives (active religious belief)
2. Historical Arabian folklore (pre-Islamic and Islamic era)
3. Western literary adaptations (Arabian Nights translations)
4. Modern popular culture (films, TV, games)
